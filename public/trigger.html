Introduction of
Asthma Triggers

There are several actions an asthma patient can do to reduce the possibility of asthma attacks. The first is to limit or reduce the triggers that lead to an attack. Triggers can increase the probability of asthma attacks and aggravate the severity of asthma.

What is trigger?

Some Asthma is genetic, however, many common physical and environmental factors can increase the probability and aggravate the severity of asthma attacks. These are called triggers.

What can I do about trigger?

It is not possible to completely avoid triggers, but you can reduce your exposure to environmental triggers and plan for physical factors.

Is everyone sensitive to all the triggers?

No. People are often sensitive to more than one trigger, but they may have different responses. The severity of the allergic reaction also varies between people.

What is the effect of contacting triggers?

Triggers can cause the airways and lungs to become narrow and inflamed, leading to asthma symptoms.

Bushfire

Bushfire may contain much harmful smoke and dust which will trigger asthma.


Hormone

Studies have shown that female hormone disorder has a significant impact on asthma attack


Pets

Cats, dogs and birds can generate certain kinds of protein, touching or breathing them will disturb your immune system, leading to allergic reactions.


Pollen

Many people are allergic to wind-borne pollen from grasses, weeds and trees. Once these pollens blown into nose and eyes, asthma and allergies may attack.
