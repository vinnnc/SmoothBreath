ASTHMA RESEARCH

The general visualization of asthma can be found below. This information is shown through bar chart, pie chart and severity of asthma. Scroll down to explore more.

Rural and Urban Area Female Age Distribution

As one of the chronic diseases, many women have asthma. However, the incidence of asthma is varying from different locations and age groups. The bar chart shows the incidence of asthmatics in both rural and urban Victoria. The red bar represents Rural area whereas the dark blue bar is for the urban. In all age groups, the incidence of asthma in the rural area are higher than urban, especially in the age of 25 to 34.

Asthma Incidence in Different Age Group of Female People

In Rural Victoria, there are approximately 11,000 females facing problems with asthma, under 65 years. In age group 25 to 34, there are almost 30,000 patients. Followed by females aged 45 to 54 years old, which have around 20,000 patients. Compared to these two age groups, the people aged 18 to 24 years seem to have the least cases of asthma.
