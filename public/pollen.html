overall

Pollen is a common trigger of asthma, it can spark off an allergic reaction in susceptible people. In the case of asthma, the lungs are affected, bringing on the symptoms of wheeze, cough, chest tightness and difficulty in breathing.

Pollen causes more of an impact in Australia during spring and early summer than at other times of the year.

English Oak

A species of flowering plant in the beech and oak family, Fagaceae. It is native to most of Europe west of the Caucasus.Deciduous. 20-30m. Slow growing. Stout erect trunk with open crown to 30m. Shiny dark green lobed leaves. Flowers are pendulous catkins, and fruit is an acorn. A cold climate tree.

  • Pollen Calendar: March to May

Lemon Bottlebrush

The entire genus is endemic to Australia but widely cultivated in many other regions and naturalised in scattered locations. Their status as a separate taxon is in doubt, some authorities accepting that the difference between callistemons and melaleucas is not sufficient for them to be grouped in a separate genus. Evergreen 6m with 3m spread. Trunk erect with a rough bark. Leaves light narrow needle like. Flowers in dense red spikes.

  • Pollen Calendar: September

Lesser Canary-grass

Densely tufted. Erect 1.5m or taller the stems have a bulbous swelling at the base and the leaves are long and flat. The flowering head is cylindrical and dense. This grass is used to stabilise pastures and eroded areas and is a roadside.

  • Pollen Calendar: December, January, February

Shade Pellitory

This weed grows up to 60cm high and has a woody red hairy stem and hairy alternate leaves. Florets grow in the axils of leaves closely applied to the stem and are white to pink in colour. This is an urban weed colonising the lower North Shore of Sydney but spreading to inner-city areas. It has been reported in Melbourne, Adelaide, Fremantle.

  • Pollen Calendar: October, December, January

Timothy Grass

Perennial up to 1.5m forming large clumps. The leaves are hairless and dense and about 6-9mm wide. The flowering head is spike-like and cylindrical 5-15cm long. It is sown as a pasture and hay grass in cooler areas.

  • Pollen Calendar: September to December
