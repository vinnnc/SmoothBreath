overall

Pollen is a common trigger of asthma, it can spark off an allergic reaction in susceptible people. For asthmatics, pollen affects the lungs, with symptoms of wheezing, coughing, chest tightness and difficulty in breathing.

Pollen causes more of an impact in Australia during spring and early summer than at other times of the year.

We have provided a map below so you can see where some of the most common pollen-producing plants are within Victoria. High-density areas with red and purple and you can filter results by a specific plant if you believe it may be one of your triggers.

If you have attacked by pollen, please record it through our application which will be helpful for your medical treatment.

How to deal with pollen:

  • Stay away from pollen source as much as possible - we realise that this is not always possible when you are surrounded by agricultural land and natural bush.
  • Take your inhaler with you.
  • Identify any specific pollen-producing plants and stay aware of their most active months.
  • If you are having more frequent and/ or severe attacks, you may want to consider wearing a mask when working or exercising outdoors.

English Oak

Deciduous or evergreen trees, up to 25 to 30 m or more. Bark grayish brown, slightly smooth. Branchlets brown, glabrous. Leaf blade ovate or elliptic, 10 -- 20 cm long, apex acuminate, base cuneate, dark green, back pale green, glabrous or tufted in vein axils. Male inflorescence and leaves open at the same time, several clusters; Female flowers solitary or 2-3 on total stalk about 1 cm long. Cup-shaped, containing 1/4 -- 1/3 nuts, 1.5 -- 1.8 cm in diameter, 1 -- 1.2 cm in height; Bracteoles triangular, densely imbricate, glabrous and glossy. Nuts oblong, ca. 1.5 cm in diameter, 2 -- 2.5 cm tall, light brown, thinly tomentose, later deciduous; Round at the top, with small pillars.

  • Pollen Calendar: March to May

Lemon Bottlebrush

Lemon Bottlebrush, labial, perennial herbs. The whole plant is smooth, with yellow glands dotted with dark green, usually slightly purple, especially at the edges of the leaves. margin is serrated. The purple calyx has very distinct yellow glandular lines. It is also sometimes used to make lemon-flavored tea drinks and has certain medicinal value. Leaf light green, because knead can produce lemon aroma and get the name. The whole plant has a fresh fragrance.

  • Pollen Calendar: September

Lesser Canary-grass

The Lesser Canary-grass is tall and stocky, with a straight, glabrous stem, usually two to six branches from the root. The tongue is distinct and membranous, 0.6-0.8 cm long, rounded at the tip. Leaf blade slanted, 8.9 ~ 25.4 cm long, 0.6 ~ 1.9 cm wide, flat, upper and lower surfaces are often rough, midrib is thick green. Panicles compact and firm, sometimes slightly diffuse, trunk 7.6 to 40.6 cm long, usually 3 to 16 branches, branches 1.2 to 3.8 cm long. The clusters are dense, with inflorescence green or mauve at first growth and yellowish brown at maturity. The glume is white and green.

  • Pollen Calendar: December, January, February

Shade Pellitory

Herbs, sparsely subshrubs. Leaves alternate, entire, 3-veined or 3-veined, stalactite punctate; Stipule. Inflorescences axillary, often consisting of a few flowers, shortly sessile or sessile; Bracts calyx-shaped, striate. Flowers hybrid, bisexual: perianth slice 4 deep split, valvate arrangement; Stamens 4. Male flower: perianth slice 4; Stamens 4. Female flowers: tepals 4, united into tubular, 4-lobed; Ovary erect; Style short or absent; The head of a brush or spoon; Stamens do not exist. Achene ovate, slightly compressed, pericarp shelled, shiny, enveloped in persistent perianth. Seeds endosperm, cotyledons oblong-ovate.

  • Pollen Calendar: October, December, January

Timothy Grass

Herbs, perennial. Fibrous roots dense, with short rhizome. Culms erect, basally often bulbous and persistent with withered leaf sheaths, 40-120 cm tall, with 5-6 nodes.Leaf sheath flabby, shorter than or lower than internodes, smooth glabrous; Lobular membranous, 2-5 mm long; Leaf blade flat, both surfaces and margin rough, 10-30 cm long, 3-8 mm wide.Panicles terete, grayish green, 4-15 cm long, 5-6 mm wide; Spikelets oblong; Glume membranous, ca. 3 mm long, 3-veined, spine with stiffly ciliated, apex truncated, tip 0.5-1 mm long; Lemma membranous, ca. 2 mm long, with 7 veins, veins suprabrous, apex obtuse; Palea slightly shorter than lemma; Anthers ca. 1.5 mm.

  • Pollen Calendar: September to December
